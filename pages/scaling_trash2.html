世界樹の黄金杖 = Golden Staff of the World Tree

This one is interesting, this is likely a reference to Etrian Odyssey, the name mentions 世界樹 or the World Tree. It is a generalized form of many myths of great trees that connect the world found in many cultures. But this concept is used as a key term in Etrain Odyssey, the Japanese title even has it in there: 世界樹 の迷宮. This acts as the only real concrete connection lore wise, and funnily enough, this precendent was created in Persona, so it would be consistent to Megaten if that were the case.

Here Elizabeth is possessed by Zeus and he makes clear references that the world he comes from is the Etrian Odyssey world, such as mentioning the Yggdrasil which is important the titular World Tree, and the cheeky inclusion of odyssey. But we don't need to speculate as in "Persona Q: Shadow of the Labyrinth Official Visual Materials," this is said.

"This one, referred to by Director Kaneda as the "winged" version, is modeled after Zeus as a celestial god embodying lust and arrogance. In this title, Zeus was designed under the setting that he is one of twelve exceptional, elite Andros created during the great war against the Deep Ones (Fukabito). Those with a keen eye will likely recognize that he is based on the archetype of a certain NPC from Etrian Odyssey III.(Hyūga)"

The Andros or Yggdroids in English are a type of robot in Etrian Odyssey III: The Drowned City. As it is stated here, this Zeus is meant to be from Etrain Odyssey.

So either way, either through Persona or by itself, there is a connection.

Large Swords

Syria is real in Metaphor confirmed.

Cursed Blade Nihil is the name and this is actually a continued tradition of referencing Osamu Tezuka's manga Dororo. The SMT card even mentions this is a refrence the late great Osamu Tezuka, isnt that neat. The spelling is a unique Megaten thing from what I could find. Although, weirdly it's used a lot in Etrain Odyssey.

All of these sword are referencing swords from Shinto tales, while nothing too specific to Megaten titles of the past like unqiue spelling or something (there could be visual similarity but it's so hard to find footage that I can only guess), all of these have made appearances in Megaten titles so I think it adds more, consistency, so to say. That it got that philosophy, it knows how to ball with da' Megaten.

The first one is referencing the Kusanagi no Tsurugi (天叢雲剣), you may notice the spelling is different but it's negligable in this case as the part that is different is the sword (太刀 instead of 剣) part of the kanji to specify it is in the large sword class. Which will be funny later because the next two do not do that so get fucked Yamato Takeru, the guy associated with using it. Fun fact about that guy though, he has appeared as Naoto's 2nd tier persona and as a demon in SMT IV, I don't want to link to those because it'd take 1% more work so you are just gonna have to look it up or trust me with your life. Also fun fact, all of these swords are related to Susanoo, in this case he found the sword.

Next up is the Totsuka no Tsurugi, similar story as before, sans spelling, it da' same. Although, one interesting factoid is that this sword is the ultimate sword in P4 due to the story where Izanagi uses one to slice up Hinokagutsuchi after he kills his wife, Izanami, in childbirth. It appears in other Megaten titles, should be noted. Such as SMT V, as it appears as the sword the Nahobino summons out of his hand, isn't that swell. In the case for V, it is referencing the fact that Susanoo uses one of These swords to kill Orochi.

Lastly is the Ame no Habakiri (天羽々斬), which is the proper name for the blade used to slay Orochi and just like the other two appears as a weapon in the series. Side note, getting any footage of the name for this weapon in Persona 5 Strikers was a nightmare. It is spelt in Katakana for some reason but it means the same thing.

アメ= Ame ノ= no ハバキリ = Habakiri
